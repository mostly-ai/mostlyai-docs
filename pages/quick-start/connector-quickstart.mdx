# Quick start: Connectors

Connectors are the resources that contain the connection details needed to connect to a database or a cloud storage bucket.

Follow these instructions to create a new connector. You can share connectors with other MOSTLY AI users by making them public so others can use them to create [generators](/quick-start/model-creators), [datasets](/quick-start/dataset-quickstart), and [synthetic datasets](/quick-start/data-consumers).

<Steps>
## Step 1: Create a connector

<Tabs items={['Manual', 'Assistant', 'Synthetic Data SDK']}>
<Tabs.Tab>

1. On the MOSTLY AI platform, open **Connectors** from the left-side navigation menu.
2. Select the connector template you want to use and provide the connection details. MOSTLY AI offers the following templates:
    - [Apache Hive](/connectors/apache-hive)
    - [Azure](/connectors/azure)
    - [BigQuery](/connectors/bigquery)
    - [Databricks](/connectors/databricks)
    - [Google Cloud](/connectors/google-cloud)
    - [Maria DB](/connectors/mariadb)
    - [MySQL](/connectors/mysql)
    - [Oracle](/connectors/oracle)
    - [PostgreSQL](/connectors/postgresql)
    - [S3](/connectors/s3)
    - [Snowflake](/connectors/snowflake)
    - [SQL Server](/connectors/sql-server)

3. Click **Save**.

The connector is now available on the **Connectors** page.

**What's next**

You can now use the connector to create a new [dataset](/quick-start/dataset-quickstart), train a [generator](/quick-start/model-creators), or explore with the [Assistant](/assistant).

**What's next**

After you've created a connector, you can use it to train a generator, create a dataset, or generate synthetic data. You can also transfer it to an organization.

## Step 2: Share the dataset with your organization

1. Open the connector from the **Connectors** page.
2. Click **Share** in the upper-right corner.
3. Select the organization to which to share the resource using the **Owner** dropdown and click **Save**.

The connector is now available to all members of your organization and they can use it to create a generator or other [artifacts](/assistant/artifacts).

**What's next**

You can make the connector public so that it is available to all logged-in users in the platform.

## Step 3: Make the dataset public

If your connector might be of interest to other users, you can make it public. Be cautious not to share connectors which may allow access to data which contain any sensitive information.

1. Open the connector from the **Connectors** page.
2. Click **Share**.
3. Select **Public** from the **Visibility** dropdown and click **Save**.

**Result**

Public connectors of organizations are listed on the organization's profile page.

They are also available on the **Connectors** page for all logged-in users.

**What's next**

To create a generator, see [Quickstart: Generators](model-creators).
